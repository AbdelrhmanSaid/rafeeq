# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Everyday Muslims, mobile-first. Arabic-speaking users who open Rafeeq several times a day on their phone — checking prayer times, reading azkar after salah, dipping into the Quran in spare minutes. Sessions are frequent and short, often on the go, and the app is used as an installed PWA as much as in the browser. The interface is Arabic and RTL-first.

## Product Purpose

Rafeeq (رفيق — "companion") is a Muslim companion app that gathers daily worship tools in one place: prayer times, Quran reading and recitation, azkar, qibla, sebha, zakat calculation, and radio. Success is measured in depth of worship — the quality of the experience while in use: focus, tranquility, and completeness — not in engagement metrics or session counts.

## Positioning

All worship, one calm place. Everything a Muslim needs daily — prayers, Quran, azkar, qibla — in one fast, serene app. Every screen should reinforce that calm completeness.

## Brand Personality

Modern, clean, minimal. The interface should evoke tranquility and focus (a quiet, distraction-free space for worship), warmth and companionship (friendly and familiar, true to the name رفيق), and lightness and ease (effortless in the small daily moments it serves).

## Anti-references

- Cluttered Islamic apps: ad-heavy, ornament-stuffed interfaces where decoration buries the content.
- Generic SaaS dashboards: sterile admin-panel look — stat tiles, card grids, corporate coldness.
- Over-gamified apps: streaks, badges, and dopamine mechanics that cheapen worship.
- Heavy skeuomorphic mushaf: faux leather, paper textures, literal book metaphors.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm is the feature.** Quiet layouts, restrained color, no competing noise. When in doubt, remove.
2. **The content is sacred; the chrome is quiet.** Quran text, azkar, and prayer times lead every screen; UI recedes into support.
3. **Fast in small moments.** Design for 30-second sessions on a phone: the primary task of each screen reachable instantly, one-handed.
4. **Warm minimalism.** Modern and clean, but never sterile — warmth comes through type, spacing, and tone, not ornament.
5. **Depth over engagement.** Never optimize for time-in-app or compulsive return; optimize for focus and completeness during worship.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort: no formal WCAG target, but avoid obvious accessibility failures (unreadable contrast, keyboard traps, missing labels). The app already ships user-controlled font scaling and light/dark modes; keep both working well.
